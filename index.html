Kyle Crouse

Hello, I am Kyle. Developer of fine web programs to those whom I work with and to everyone else a beer-enthusiast, music-lover, traveler and occasional cruise director.

As titles go, I am currently a Principal Design Technologist for frog in NY. My primary role is as a lead front-end developer with a passion for accessible, progressive HTML, CSS and JavaScript. I develop rapid prototypes, corporate sites and web applications.

As tech lead on a recent project, I devised a Frankenstack comprised of Wordpress, NodeJS, MongoDB and Redis to deliver a modular, scalable website complete with RESTful API for future feature integration.

I helped design and build a web-based mobile scavenger hunt for use at CES, featuring mobile checkpoints throughout Las Vegas and interactive TV panels depicting real-time game activity and leaderboards... all in a span of three weeks.

I joined the Obama campaign in 2008 as a front-end developer for the New Media Design team in Chicago, where I built many sites and worked cheek-to-cheek with talented designers (in a 3x5 cubical) to help get our Man elected.

I often dabble with new HTML5 techniques by recreating classic games and game shows such as Family Feud, JEOPARDY! and others. I’ve started an underground radio network and I’ve hosted and developed competitions for online racing simulation games.
